Decision Support Systems

Equipping students with the theory and application of systems that aid in organizational decision-making.

Course Description

This course explores the fundamental concepts, methodologies, and technologies utilized in building and deploying Decision Support Systems (DSS). Students will learn how organizations leverage data-driven approaches to enhance strategic and tactical decision-making. The curriculum covers key components of DSS, including data warehousing, business intelligence (BI), online analytical processing (OLAP), and data mining. Emphasis is placed on practical application, enabling students to design and conceptualize DSS solutions for real-world business problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the architecture and components of a Decision Support System.
  • Analyze business requirements to design effective DSS solutions.
  • Apply data warehousing and business intelligence concepts to support analytics.
  • Evaluate and select appropriate data mining techniques for knowledge discovery.
  • Develop a conceptual prototype of a DSS for a given business case.
  • Critically assess the ethical implications of data-driven decision-making.

Key Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Managerial Decision Making and BI
  • Data Warehousing: Architecture and Implementation
  • Business Performance Management (BPM)
  • Data Mining for Business Analytics
  • Predictive Modeling and Text Mining
  • Big Data Analytics and Future Trends in DSS
