Teaching

Core courses taught in the Information Systems study program.

Emphasis on conceptual understanding and practical application.
DSS Decision Support Systems

Decision Support Systems

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to decision support systems (DSS), covering fundamental concepts, architectures, and methodologies for improving organizational decision-making.

Students explore multi-criteria decision making (MCDM) techniques including AHP, TOPSIS, and SAW, alongside model-driven and data-driven approaches to support planning and strategy.

  • DSS concepts and architectures.
  • MCDM methods (AHP, TOPSIS, SAW).
  • Model-driven and data-driven DSS.
  • Decision analysis and visualization.
ML Machine Learning

Machine Learning

This course introduces fundamental machine learning concepts and practical techniques for extracting insights from data through modeling and analysis.

Students gain hands-on experience in data wrangling, preprocessing, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and the development of predictive models using various machine learning algorithms.

  • Data wrangling and preprocessing.
  • Exploratory data analysis (EDA).
  • Predictive modeling and ML algorithms.
  • Data visualization and storytelling.
DX Digital Transformation

Digital Transformation

This course examines the strategic dimensions of digital transformation in modern organizations, and how emerging technologies and information systems drive innovation and competitive advantage.

Students analyze digital transformation frameworks, business model innovation, digital strategy development, fuzzy decision-making under uncertainty, and change management practices in the digital era.

  • Digital strategy and business model innovation.
  • Fuzzy decision making.
  • Digital transformation frameworks and methodologies.
  • Organizational change in the digital era.
