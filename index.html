About

Teaching, research, and mentoring in Information Systems.

Background

With a strong foundation in both technology and pedagogy, my academic focus bridges systems analysis, digital transformation, and the effective management of IT projects.

I am currently seeking PhD opportunities to investigate the socio-technical dimensions of digital inclusion. I am particularly interested in how state-led digital initiatives are experienced, adapted, and resisted in fragile infrastructural settings across Southeast Asia.

Focus Areas
  • Researching the gap between universal digital policy ambitions and ground-level realities.
  • Teaching core courses in Decision Support Systems, IT Project Management, and Digital Transformation.
  • Managing DSLab-ID, fostering practical skills in Data Science & DSS.
  • Actively supervising undergraduate theses and developing project-based learning materials.

Research

Research & Publications

Exploring the intersections of digital inclusion, socio-technical systems, and data-driven decision making.

01

Digital Inclusion & Policy Realities

Investigating how state-led digital initiatives (e.g., upskilling schemes under Indonesia's Satu Data framework) are adapted and resisted in fragile infrastructural settings, addressing the gap between policy ambitions and ground-level realities.

02

Decision Support Systems & MCDM

Development and application of multi-criteria decision making methods — AHP, TOPSIS, and related approaches — for practical decision problems in business and organizational contexts.

03

Educational Data Mining & Analytics

Applying predictive modeling, statistical analysis, and data warehouse design to institutional research, focusing on learning analytics and academic performance prediction to support strategic decision-making.
