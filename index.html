About Me

With a profound passion for both technology and pedagogy, my academic focus is centered on systems analysis and digital transformation in the business sector, and the effective management of information technology projects to achieve organizational objectives.

I am dedicated to mentoring students to become competent information systems professionals, fully equipped to meet the challenges of the contemporary industry.

Teaching

I teach core courses in information systems, Decision Support System, Machine Learning, and Digital Transformation at the undergraduate level at Atma Jaya Catholic University.

Undergraduate

Decision Support Systems

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to decision support systems (DSS), covering fundamental concepts, architectures, and methodologies for enhancing organizational decision-making. Students explore multi-criteria decision making (MCDM) techniques including AHP, TOPSIS, and SAW, alongside model-driven and data-driven approaches. The course emphasizes practical application of DSS frameworks, decision analysis, and visualization techniques to solve complex business problems and support strategic planning in modern information systems environments.

  • DSS concepts and architecture
  • MCDM methods (AHP, TOPSIS, SAW)
  • Model-driven and data-driven DSS
  • Decision analysis and visualization
Undergraduate

Machine Learning

This course introduces fundamental machine learning concepts and practical techniques for extracting insights from data. Students gain hands-on experience in data wrangling, preprocessing, and exploratory data analysis (EDA) while developing skills in predictive modeling and machine learning algorithms. The curriculum emphasizes real-world applications in organizational contexts, covering supervised and unsupervised learning methods, model evaluation, and effective data visualization for storytelling and decision support.

  • Data wrangling and preprocessing
  • Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
  • Predictive modeling and machine learning
  • Data visualization and storytelling
Undergraduate

Digital Transformation

This course examines the strategic dimensions of digital transformation in contemporary organizations, exploring how emerging technologies and information systems drive innovation and competitive advantage. Students analyze digital transformation frameworks, business model innovation, and implementation methodologies while investigating topics such as digital strategy development, fuzzy decision-making for uncertain environments, and change management practices. The course integrates MCDM approaches and analytical modeling to address complex transformation challenges in the digital era.

    Comprehensive study of digital transformation strategies, implementation frameworks, and emerging technologies that drive organizational innovation and competitive advantage in the digital era.
  • Digital strategy and business model innovation
  • Fuzzy decision making
  • Digital transformation frameworks and methodologies
