Course Overview

This course provides comprehensive understanding of Decision Support Systems (DSS) and Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) methods. Students will learn to analyze complex problems, model decision scenarios, and implement various MCDM techniques including AHP, TOPSIS, SAW, and WP.

Through practical exercises and real-world case studies, students will develop the ability to design and implement decision support systems for various domains including business, healthcare, education, and public policy.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand fundamental concepts of Decision Support Systems and their applications
  • Master multiple MCDM methods: AHP, TOPSIS, SAW, WP, ELECTRE, and PROMETHEE
  • Analyze decision problems and identify appropriate criteria and alternatives
  • Implement DSS using Python, Excel, and specialized software
  • Evaluate and validate decision models with sensitivity analysis
  • Apply DSS methodologies to real-world business and organizational problems

Course Topics

Week 1-2: DSS Fundamentals

Introduction to DSS, decision-making processes, types of decisions, and DSS architecture

Week 3-4: Analytic Hierarchy Process

AHP methodology, pairwise comparison, consistency ratio, and priority calculation

Week 5-6: TOPSIS Method

Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution with normalization techniques

Week 7-8: SAW & Weighted Methods

Simple Additive Weighting and other weighted aggregation approaches

Week 9-10: Advanced MCDM

ELECTRE, PROMETHEE, and hybrid decision-making methods

Week 11-12: Implementation

Building DSS applications with Python, web-based DSS, and system integration

Week 13: Validation & Analysis

Sensitivity analysis, robustness testing, and result interpretation

Week 14: Final Project

Comprehensive DSS project presentation and evaluation
